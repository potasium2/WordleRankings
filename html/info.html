Hello, you're probably wondering—"Why did you quantify an over 70 day streak of Wordle games, played by some friends and other people you have never met, into an online ranked leaderboard system?"—And to be quite honest I don't really know.

This initially started as coding 'practice' in C# where I was just simply making an algorithm to 'quantify an over 70 day streak of Wordle games'. I ended up moving everything over to HTML and Javascript as a part of web dev practice, mostly because I am taking an HTML and CSS class next semester, some I used this as an opportunity to get some practice in.

Doesn't really answer the question of "why Wordle?" to say it directly; because why not. The data for the games was there, and it was something I was playing and getting a pretty good streak in so making a rating system seemed fun and I wanted to see where I would place. I'm not sure how some people would view me currently As of 01/08/2025 being rank #1 as. That being whether I made the system around me being rank 1 or cheated to get there. In any case, all of this was made in good fun, none of it should be treated as undeniable proof that I, Lifeline/potasium_/Noah, am the best Wordle player on the planet, just something I made for fun that other people can view and compete in for fun :)

I also want to use this page as like a means of answering some questions people may have, so heres like a quick Q&A of sorts:

Q: What does WR stand for?

A: WR stands for Wordle Rating. I know it looks like 'World Record' but between that, Performance Points, and Competitive Points, I think I made the right choice.

Q: What is the value between rank and a players profile picture?

A: That is the rank change, the furthest it looks back is 2 weeks. If a person has joined less than 2 weeks ago then it looks back to the day that person first joined.

Q: What are the "Old Rankings"?

A: The Old Leaderboard is the original iteration of the rating system. It was heavily unbalanced and favored playing on days where a lot of people played. It's mostly just there for comparison sake.

Q: What is #1 Count?

A: #1 Count is how many times you achieved the best guess count out of everyone for a day, this can be tied with multiple people, so it is possible for this count to go up for multiple people in a day.

Q: What do the background colors on the rankings page signify?

A: There are three colors that a person can have on their ranking banner: Active, Inactive, and Warning.

Active means that a person is active and has played the wordle within' the last 30 days.

Inactive means that a person has not played the Wordle within' the last 31-60 days. If a player is inactive for more than 60 days than their banner will be removed from the rankings listing.

Warning means that a person has confirmed cheated on a previous Wordle. If a player gets 'tagged' more than 3 times in a short period then their name will be temporarily removed from the rankings listing. Being tagged and 'banned' will not prevent someone from still gaining rating, it will just remove them from the leaderboards.

A: Mostly just to keep track of things as I add them, for the most part you can ignore it but I have it setup as Major_Release.Minor_Release.Patch_Release. Major Releases being for overall site changes Large CSS changes, New Site features, etc. Minor Releases being for additions to Major Releases New Leaderboard Additions, New Site Pages, etc. and Patch Releases being for bug fixes and such.
